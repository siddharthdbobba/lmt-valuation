## The ask

Buy Lockheed Martin at roughly $494 for a 12-month target near $560, about 16% total return including the dividend, in a low-beta defensive name with limited downside. This is not a high-growth re-rating story. It is an asymmetric, cash-generative compounder riding a multi-year global defense up-cycle, where the base case offers mid-teens upside and the conservative case is roughly flat, so the risk/reward skews favorably to the long side.

## Business

Lockheed Martin is the largest Western defense prime by revenue (about $75B FY2025) across four segments:
- **Aeronautics (~40%)** anchored by the F-35, the largest weapons program in history.
- **Rotary and Mission Systems (~23%)** including Sikorsky helicopters, Aegis combat systems, sensors, and training.
- **Missiles and Fire Control (~19%)** including PAC-3, HIMARS, GMLRS, and JASSM, the segment most levered to munitions replenishment.
- **Space (~17%)** including strategic missiles, satellites, hypersonics, and the Next Generation Interceptor.

The company carries a backlog of roughly $170B, near 2x annual revenue, which gives unusual forward revenue visibility for an industrial.

## Thesis: three pillars

**1. A munitions super-cycle on top of a record backlog.** Elevated global defense budgets (NATO 2%-plus commitments, European rearmament, Indo-Pacific deterrence) and the drawdown-and-replenish dynamic from recent conflicts drive sustained demand, especially in Missiles and Fire Control. A ~$170B backlog converts that demand into low-cyclicality, mid-single-digit revenue growth.

**2. The F-35 is a multi-decade, margin-accretive annuity.** Even if production volumes flatten, the installed base keeps growing toward a 3,000-plus jet program of record. Sustainment, upgrades, and modernization (TR-3, Block 4) are stickier and higher-margin than new builds, shifting the program's revenue mix toward recurring, defensible cash flow over time.

**3. A cash-return compounder.** Strong unlevered free cash flow funds a growing dividend (about 2.7% yield) and consistent buybacks that have steadily shrunk the share count to roughly 232M. That compounds per-share value even with modest top-line growth, and the low beta makes LMT defensive ballast in a risk-off tape.

## Valuation: how I get to ~$560

The model triangulates three methods (WACC about 6.8% via CAPM):
- **DCF, Gordon growth:** ~$581 (about 18% upside). Five-year unlevered FCF with a 2.25% perpetuity growth, below nominal GDP.
- **DCF, exit multiple (12x EV/EBITDA):** ~$482 (about flat). A deliberately conservative anchor that defines the downside.
- **Comparable companies:** ~$606 median, cross-checking EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, and P/E against RTX, Northrop Grumman, General Dynamics, and L3Harris.

The blended base case is about $556. I weight toward the Gordon-growth DCF and comps because they better capture a high-quality, long-duration cash-flow business, and I set the target at ~$560. The key point is the asymmetry: the bull and base cases cluster around $560 to $606, while the conservative exit-multiple case still holds near today's price, so downside is limited.

## Catalysts (next 6-18 months)

- FY2026 defense appropriations and the NDAA, plus any supplemental aid packages.
- Missiles and Fire Control backlog conversion as PAC-3 and HIMARS production ramps.
- Resumption and pace of F-35 TR-3 deliveries and Block 4 modernization.
- Continued buyback execution and the annual dividend increase.
- Program wins in Space (Next Generation Interceptor, hypersonics).

## Risks and what would make me wrong

- **Budget and political risk:** continuing resolutions, a debt-ceiling-driven sequester, or a shift in spending priorities would pressure awards and sentiment.
- **F-35 concentration:** sustainment-cost scrutiny, foreign-order timing, or further TR-3 and Block 4 delays hit the single most important program.
- **Fixed-price development losses:** classified and development programs have produced margin charges before and can again.
- **Multiple compression:** as a low-growth defensive, LMT can de-rate if rates rise or the market rotates aggressively into risk.

A sustained break below the low-$480s on a real budget-cut catalyst, or evidence that F-35 sustainment economics are deteriorating, would invalidate the thesis.

## Bottom line

Lockheed Martin offers mid-teens total return with limited downside, backed by a record backlog, a multi-decade F-35 annuity, and a disciplined cash-return program, all in a low-beta defensive wrapper. Buy.
