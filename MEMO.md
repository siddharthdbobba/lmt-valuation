# Lockheed Martin (LMT) Investment Memo

## Thesis

Lockheed Martin is a high-quality defense prime with durable demand from long-cycle programs, a large funded backlog, and strong exposure to U.S. and allied defense modernization. The stock screens as a Hold to modest Buy in this model because the DCF and peer cross-check point to upside versus the recent market price, but the case depends on stable margins, budget support, and execution on major programs.

## Business Overview

Lockheed Martin operates through four segments: Aeronautics, Rotary and Mission Systems, Missiles and Fire Control, and Space. Aeronautics is the largest segment and is anchored by the F-35 program, while Missiles and Fire Control benefits from demand for air defense, precision fires, and tactical missiles. Rotary and Mission Systems includes Sikorsky helicopters, naval combat systems, sensors, and training, and Space includes strategic missiles, satellites, missile warning, and classified programs.

## Valuation Summary

The model uses FY2025 10-K financials as the base year and forecasts five years of revenue, EBIT, cash taxes, D&A, capex, and working capital to calculate unlevered free cash flow. The DCF includes both a Gordon growth terminal value and an exit EV/EBITDA terminal value, with the sensitivity table showing the implied share price across WACC and terminal growth assumptions. Comparable companies are used as a cross-check with aerospace and defense peers including RTX, Northrop Grumman, General Dynamics, L3Harris, and Boeing.

Based on the workbook output (FY2025 base-year inputs, WACC of roughly 6.8 percent, and 2.25 percent perpetuity growth), the DCF returns an implied share price of about 581 dollars under the Gordon growth method and about 482 dollars under a 12x exit EV/EBITDA, framing a fair-value range of roughly 480 to 580 dollars. The comparable-company cross-check (median peer EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, and P/E) implies roughly 606 dollars. Blending the DCF and comps base cases gives a target of about 556 dollars, or roughly 13 percent above the recent price of about 494 dollars. The model should be refreshed against the latest 10-K, 10-Q, market price, beta, Treasury yield, and peer multiples before being used in any formal setting.

## Recommendation

Hold, with a target of about 556 dollars and clear upside to a Buy if the perpetuity-growth case holds. The base case supports a constructive view on LMT, but roughly 13 percent blended upside is not enough to ignore execution risk, political budget risk, and valuation sensitivity to WACC and terminal growth. A reasonable interview-ready framing is that Lockheed is a defensive compounder with visible cash flows, but not a high-growth rerating story.

## Key Risks

- U.S. defense budget and appropriations delays could pressure awards, cash conversion, and investor sentiment.
- F-35 program concentration creates risk if production, sustainment, pricing, or political support weakens.
- Margin pressure from fixed-price development contracts, inflation, supply chain constraints, and labor availability could reduce free cash flow.
- Geopolitical demand supports the backlog, but timing of international orders and export approvals can be uneven.
